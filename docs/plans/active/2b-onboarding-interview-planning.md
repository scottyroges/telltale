# Plan 2b: Onboarding + Interview Planning

**Status:** Not Started
**Created:** 2026-02-28
**Revised:** 2026-03-01
**Depends on:** 2a (core memory block, cross-interview memory)
**Blocks:** 2c (post-interview processing pipeline)

## Goal

Add a **structured intake** and **LLM-generated interview plan** as an alternative path alongside the existing question catalog. When a user creates a book, they answer short prompts about ~10 life areas (childhood, career, relationships, etc.). The system feeds those responses into an LLM that generates a personalized set of interview topics and bootstraps the core memory block — so interview #1 already knows the subject.

Both flows coexist so we can experiment with which works better: the catalog-driven approach (user picks topics) vs. the onboarding-driven approach (system learns the person and suggests topics).

This plan also introduces a **three-level topic model** (Life Area → Topic → Thread) that separates coverage strategy from interview-level planning from in-interview follow-ups. Life areas become the universal organizing vocabulary — the question catalog's `category` field is migrated to `lifeAreaKey` so both flows use the same taxonomy.

## Why This Matters

Today, the user's entry point after creating a book is a page with a topic text input and a question catalog. The catalog works, but it has limitations:

1. **It puts the burden on the user.** They have to decide what to talk about before the system knows anything about them.
2. **The first interview is blind.** Despite the core memory block (2a), interview #1 starts with an empty memory because there's nothing to seed it.
3. **Generic questions miss what's unique.** A catalog can't anticipate that *your* childhood was defined by military moves or that *your* career story is really a relationship story.
4. **No coverage planning.** There's no mechanism to ensure important ground gets covered across interviews.

Onboarding addresses all four: the system learns enough to plan, the core memory block gets bootstrapped before interview #1, topics are personalized, and there's a plan that tracks what's been covered. But the catalog may still be valuable as a simpler, lower-friction entry point — so we keep both and see which users prefer.

## Why Structured Intake, Not a Conversation

The original plan used a free-form LLM conversation for onboarding. We replaced that with a structured form because:

- **The drift problem.** A free-form conversation follows the user's energy. If someone is excited about their career, the LLM spends most exchanges on that and learns nothing about their family, childhood, or inner life.
- **The "enough" problem.** The LLM doesn't know when it has enough information to generate a good plan. A structured form has a clear completion state — you've gone through the areas.
- **Skipping is signal.** When a user skips a life area, that tells us something. A conversation can't capture what wasn't said.
- **Simpler to build.** No streaming conversation, no multi-turn context management, no "when to stop" logic. Just a form and one LLM call.

## Three-Level Topic Model

1. **Life Area** (broad) — Fixed categories defined in code. Each has a prompt the user responds to during onboarding. These are the coverage checklist and the universal organizing vocabulary — both generated topics and catalog questions are tagged with life areas. ~10 areas.

2. **Interview Topic** (medium) — Personalized topics generated by the LLM from life area responses + question catalog. "Growing up on the farm with three brothers." This is what the user sees as their interview plan. Each has a **primary life area** (for display grouping in the plan UI) and optionally **secondary life areas** (for metadata/analysis).

3. **Thread** (targeted) — Specific follow-ups that emerge during interviews. "You mentioned your dad never came to your games." These can't be planned in advance — they emerge from the conversation. Schema goes in now; Phase 2c populates them via post-interview processing. Threads are displayed in their own section, separate from the life area → topic hierarchy.

The three levels interact as a funnel: life areas guarantee coverage, topics give the user a personalized plan, and threads capture the gold that only surfaces during actual interviews.

## Plan / Progress UI

After onboarding completes, the interviews page (`/book/[bookId]/interviews`) transforms into a **plan/progress view** organized hierarchically:

```
Life Area: Childhood & Family
  └── Topic: Growing up on the farm with three brothers
      └── Interview: "Early childhood memories" (completed)
  └── Topic: Summer trips to Grandma's
      └── (no interviews yet)

Life Area: Career & Work
  └── Topic: First job at the ad agency
      └── Interview: "Breaking into advertising" (completed)
  ...

── Open Threads ──
  • "Dad never came to your games" (from Interview #3)
  • "The year you don't talk about" (from Interview #5)
```

**Life areas** that the LLM identified as important for this person are shown as top-level sections. Not all 10 areas appear — only those with topics. Each shows its topics nested underneath.

**Topics** show their status (suggested, in progress, completed, skipped) and link to any associated interviews. Starting an interview from a topic is the primary way to begin a new conversation.

**Threads** appear in a separate section — not nested under topics. They're follow-up items that emerged from interviews, displayed as a list of things to explore. Some may link to a topic, but the display is flat. (Populated by 2c; the section is empty until then.)

The existing **free-text topic input** and **question catalog** remain available (collapsed or in a secondary position) so users can always start an interview outside the plan.

## Current State

- **Book creation:** `book.create` tRPC mutation → creates a Book with title only → redirects to `/book/[bookId]/interviews`
- **Interview start:** User types a topic or picks a catalog question → `interview.start` mutation → `conversationService.startInterview()` → creates Interview, hidden topic message, non-streaming opening response
- **Core memory:** `coreMemory` text field on Book, updated every turn by `memoryService`, injected into context by `contextService`
- **Question catalog:** `Question` model has `category` string field, `BookQuestion` model joins books to questions, `QuestionCatalog` + `QuestionList` components, `question` tRPC router
- **Conversation flow:** `sendMessage` does streaming response + parallel memory update, uses `getConversationSystemPrompt()` + `getMemorySystemPrompt()`

## Architecture Decisions

### Life areas as the universal taxonomy

Life areas replace question categories as the organizing vocabulary. The `Question.category` field is migrated to `Question.lifeAreaKey`, aligning the catalog with the same taxonomy used by onboarding and generated topics. This means:
- The catalog filters by life area instead of arbitrary category strings
- The plan generation prompt can directly map catalog questions to life areas
- One vocabulary instead of two

### Life areas in code, responses in DB

Life areas are a constant array — adding/removing is a code change, not a data migration. Only user responses are persisted (`LifeAreaResponse` model). This is appropriate since these are structural categories that change rarely and need to be consistent across all users.

### Primary + secondary life areas on topics

Each `InterviewTopic` has a `primaryLifeAreaKey` (used for grouping in the plan UI) and a `lifeAreaKeys` array (all related areas, for metadata/analysis). A topic like "Your relationship with your father" has primary = `relationships` but also touches `childhood` and `challenges`. The LLM assigns both during generation.

### No InterviewPlan wrapper model

Topics belong directly to the Book via `bookId` on `InterviewTopic`. The original plan's `InterviewPlan → InterviewPlanTopic` hierarchy added indirection without benefit. A book has topics; that's the relationship.

### No Interview type enum

The original plan used `type: ONBOARDING | INTERVIEW` on Interview. Since onboarding is now a form (not a conversation), no interview is created during onboarding. No enum needed.

### Topic status transitions

Valid transitions for `TopicStatus`:

```
SUGGESTED → ACCEPTED     (user starts an interview from this topic)
SUGGESTED → SKIPPED      (user skips)
SKIPPED   → SUGGESTED    (user un-skips)
ACCEPTED  → COMPLETED    (explored enough — set by user or LLM post-processing in 2c)
COMPLETED → ACCEPTED     (user wants to revisit / add more)
```

LLM-generated topics start at `SUGGESTED`. User-created topics and catalog-added topics start at `ACCEPTED` (they've already been chosen). The service validates transitions.

### Plan generation bootstraps core memory

The plan generation LLM call produces both topics AND a core memory block. This means interview #1 starts informed — the interviewer already knows the subject's key people, life arc, and priorities. The prompt receives the current core memory as input (which may be null for new books, or rich for books with existing interviews). The LLM produces a comprehensive core memory that incorporates everything from the existing one plus new insights from the life area responses. This unconditionally replaces the existing core memory — the LLM is trusted to not lose information.

### Thread model now, population in 2c

The Thread schema goes in during Phase 1 so the data model is complete. But no code populates threads until Phase 2c (post-interview processing). Threads display in a separate section in the plan UI (not nested under topics).

### Interviews page becomes plan view

After onboarding completes, the interviews page transforms into the plan/progress view. The hierarchical life area → topic → interview structure becomes the primary way to navigate and start interviews. The catalog and free-text input remain available but move to a secondary position.

## Phase 2: Plan Generation Service + Prompt

**PR scope:** Plan generation prompt, plan generation service. No UI — testable via unit tests.

### Plan Generation Prompt

`src/prompts/plan-generation.ts` — new file, exports `getPlanGenerationPrompt(params)`.

Takes three inputs:
1. Life area responses — key → content map, noting which areas were skipped
2. Current core memory (null for new books, or existing memory from prior interviews)
3. Question catalog — all questions grouped by life area key

Produces JSON with two fields:
- `topics` — array of 8-15 topics, each with `title`, `description`, `primaryLifeAreaKey`, `lifeAreaKeys`, `rationale`
- `coreMemory` — a comprehensive Book Memory block (key people, life narrative, emotional patterns). When existing core memory is provided, the LLM incorporates it fully and adds new insights from the life area responses.

The prompt instructs the LLM to:
- Review life area responses to personalize topics
- Check question catalog for life dimensions the responses didn't cover (catalog as coverage fallback)
- Respect stated priorities — areas with longer responses matter more to the subject
- Treat skipped areas as signal (not relevant or sensitive — don't force them, but a gentle topic might be appropriate)
- Order topics: broad/foundational first (childhood, family), focused/specific later, forward-looking/thematic at the end (legacy, wisdom)
- Generate 8-15 topics
- Assign a `primaryLifeAreaKey` for display grouping and `lifeAreaKeys` for all related areas
- Produce a Book Memory section that incorporates the existing core memory (if any) plus new insights from the life area responses — no Interview Memory section since there's no active interview

### Plan Generation Service

`src/services/plan-generation.service.ts` — new file:

- `generatePlan(bookId)`:
  1. Load life area responses via `lifeAreaResponseRepository.findByBookId()`
  2. Load Book for current coreMemory via `bookRepository.findById()`
  3. Load question catalog via `questionRepository.findAll()`
  4. Call LLM with `getPlanGenerationPrompt(...)` — single non-streaming call
  5. Parse response (defensive JSON extraction, same pattern as `memoryService`)
  6. Create `InterviewTopic` records via `interviewTopicRepository.createMany()`
  7. Update `book.coreMemory` with the LLM's `coreMemory` output via `bookRepository.updateCoreMemory()` — replaces existing core memory unconditionally (the LLM was given the existing memory as input and incorporated it)
  8. Set `book.onboardingStatus` to `COMPLETE` via `bookRepository.updateOnboardingStatus()`
  9. Return the created topics

### Tests

- `tests/services/plan-generation.service.test.ts` — mock LLM, verify: topics created with correct primaryLifeAreaKey and lifeAreaKeys, core memory bootstrapped, status set, defensive parsing handles malformed LLM responses
- `tests/prompts/plan-generation.test.ts` — verify prompt includes all three inputs, questions grouped by life area

## Phase 5: Plan / Progress View (Replaces Interviews Page)

**PR scope:** Transform the interviews page into a plan/progress view after onboarding. Start interviews from topics. Catalog moves to secondary position.

### Plan View on Interviews Page

When `onboardingStatus === COMPLETE`, the interviews page transforms into the plan/progress view:

1. **Life area sections** — Each life area that has topics becomes a collapsible section. Shows the life area name and a progress indicator (e.g., "2 of 3 topics completed"). Only life areas with topics appear — not all 10.

2. **Topics under each life area** — Grouped by `primaryLifeAreaKey`. Each topic card shows:
   - Title and description
   - Status indicator: `SUGGESTED` (start), `ACCEPTED` (in progress), `COMPLETED` (done), `SKIPPED` (de-emphasized)
   - Link to associated interview(s) if any

3. **Open Threads section** — A separate section below the life areas. Shows threads as a flat list with title, description, and source interview link. Empty until 2c populates threads — section is hidden when empty.

4. **Start a new interview** — Collapsed/secondary section with the free-text topic input and question catalog (now filtered by life area key instead of category). Available for interviews outside the plan.

### Start From Topic

Clicking a suggested topic starts an interview:
- Extend `interview.start` mutation to accept optional `topicId` parameter
- When `topicId` provided: validate topic belongs to book, update topic status to `ACCEPTED`, set `topicId` on the created Interview
- Same pattern as existing `bookQuestionId` handling in the interview router — the FK lives on Interview, pointing back to the topic

### Topic Management

- Skip button on each suggested topic
- No reordering or editing for now — keep it simple

### Add Topics from Question Catalog

Users can browse the question catalog and add catalog questions as topics to their plan. This bridges the two flows — the LLM generates personalized topics, and the catalog lets users fill gaps or add things the LLM missed.

- An "Add topic" action in the plan view opens/expands the question catalog browser
- Catalog is filtered by life area key — user can browse all or filter to a specific area
- Selecting a catalog question creates an `InterviewTopic` from it: `question.title` → `topic.title`, `question.prompt` → `topic.description`, `question.lifeAreaKey` → `topic.primaryLifeAreaKey`, and the topic appears in the plan under that life area
- The new topic gets `status: ACCEPTED` (user-chosen topics skip `SUGGESTED`) and is appended to the end of its life area group
- If the life area didn't previously have any topics, a new life area section appears in the plan view

This replaces the old `BookQuestion` add-to-book flow for users who have completed onboarding. Everything in the plan is an `InterviewTopic` — whether LLM-generated or user-added from the catalog.

## Open Questions

1. **Plan mutability:** Should users be able to edit plan topic titles/descriptions, or only skip? Starting with skip only — keep it simple.
2. **Catalog vs. plan comparison:** How do we evaluate which flow is better? For now, just having both available and using them manually. Formal A/B testing or analytics is Phase 6+ territory.

## Out of Scope

- **Post-interview thread population** (Threads emerging from interviews) — this is Phase 2c territory, powered by Inngest
- **Plan evolution after interviews** (adding follow-up topics) — the plan is generated once from onboarding in 2b; 2c makes it evolve
- **Subject priorities captured formally** — priorities are captured via life area responses and reflected in the plan via the LLM, not as a separate data structure
- **Removing the question catalog** — both flows coexist; catalog deprecation is a future decision based on experimentation
- **A/B testing or analytics** comparing the two flows — that's Phase 6+ (PostHog)
- **Reordering or editing topics** — keep it simple for now
